How do historical events appear on screen?

When the 1973 oil crisis hits — how does the player experience it?

A

Newspaper front page

A full-screen modal slides in styled as a newspaper — masthead, bold headline, a short paragraph of narrative text, and the mechanical effect listed below it ("Fuel costs +40% for 6 months"). Player taps "Continue" to dismiss. Used only for major events. Minor events are just a small toast notification.

THE FARM GAZETTE · October 17, 1973
OIL EMBARGO HITS FUEL SUPPLY
OPEC nations cut exports. Diesel prices surge overnight. Farmers brace for a difficult harvest season...
⚠ Fuel costs +40% · Duration: 8 months
B

Radio broadcast banner

A banner slides down from the top of the screen styled as an old radio/TV broadcast — a small speaker icon, date, and a one-line headline. It stays visible for ~8 seconds then fades. Major events pause the game and show a brief expanded card with narrative + effects. Minor events just scroll by.

📻
BREAKING · OCT 1973
OPEC oil embargo — fuel prices surging nationwide
⚠ Fuel costs +40% for 8 months
C

Both — tiered by impact

Small events (price shifts, new products, regulations) → radio/TV banner that slides in and fades. Major events (crises, booms, generational milestones) → full newspaper front page modal that demands attention. The player learns to read the signal: banner = manageable, newspaper = this changes everything.
