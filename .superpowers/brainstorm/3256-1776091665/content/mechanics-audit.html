Realism Gaps — Priority Summary
MAJOR GAP
☁️ Climate depth
Weather is a single-day multiplier. Real farming means multi-day frost events that kill crops, accumulated drought stress, growing degree days. The foundation is there but the simulation is shallow.
MAJOR GAP
🏭 Rivals affect market
Rival farms sell crops every 7 days but prices don't react. In reality, a bumper crop year from competitors tanks your prices. This would make market strategy much more interesting.
MAJOR GAP
🧪 Processing quality
Processing is a pure ratio. No quality grades, no energy costs, no spoilage risk. Flour milled from premium wheat should be worth more. This is a missed depth layer.
MODERATE GAP
🌱 Soil chemistry (NPK)
Fertility is one number. Real soil has nitrogen for leafy growth, phosphorus for roots/fruiting, potassium for stress resistance. Crop rotation bonuses (legumes fix nitrogen) would add strategy.
MODERATE GAP
👷 Worker progression
Workers are instantly fully skilled. Skill levels that grow over time (or decline if not used) would make long-term staff decisions meaningful.
MODERATE GAP
📋 Recurring contracts
Contracts are one-off orders. A weekly supply agreement with a buyer would simulate real farm-to-table relationships and reward consistency over time.
WELL COVERED
🏦 Banking / Finance
Credit scores, DTI ratios, tiered lending, savings products, interest accrual — this is genuinely realistic already.
WELL COVERED
🐄 Animal genetics
4-gene system with trait inheritance, lactation cycles, seasonal production, optimal slaughter timing — this rivals dedicated livestock sims.
Bottom line: The game has 3 deep systems (crops, animals, production buildings, banking), 5 moderate ones, and 2 shallow ones (climate, rivals). The biggest realism wins are: making rivals move market prices, deepening climate to multi-day effects, and adding quality grades to processing output.
