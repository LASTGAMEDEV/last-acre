What Should We Add for Realism?

The game already has weather, seasons, genetics, machinery, loans, insurance, and rivals. These are the biggest gaps. Pick the one(s) that excite you most.

🌱

Soil Health & Crop Rotation

Each plot tracks NPK nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium). Over-farming the same crop depletes the soil. Rotate crops, spread fertilizer, or let fields lie fallow to recover. Bad soil → lower yields. Great soil → bonus yields.

New mechanics: soil meter per plot, fertilizer types, rotation calendar, degradation warnings

🐛

Pest & Disease Outbreaks

Specific diseases and pests target crops based on weather and season. Spreads to adjacent plots if untreated. Requires pesticide/fungicide applications, crop removal, or worker deployment to contain. Rivals can have outbreaks that affect market supply.

New mechanics: outbreak events, spread simulation, treatment options, quarantine zones

💧

Water & Irrigation Management

Crops have water requirements. Rainfall covers some of it; dry spells create deficits. Build irrigation infrastructure (sprinklers, drip lines) to fill the gap. Water costs money. Over-irrigating wastes it. Drought events hit harder without infrastructure.

New mechanics: water budget per plot, irrigation buildings, rainfall tracker, water bill

🚚

Supply Chain & Logistics Costs

Selling to distant market regions costs time and fuel. Spoilage accumulates during transit for perishables. Refrigerated trucks reduce spoilage but cost more. Fuel price fluctuates with news events. Closer markets → lower margins but faster turnover.

New mechanics: transit time, fuel costs, spoilage %, cold chain upgrades

📋

Regulatory Compliance & Certifications

Get certified organic, fair-trade, or food-safety compliant to unlock premium market prices. Certifications require audits, equipment standards, and clean records. Inspections can fail and cost fines. Competitors without certs undercut on price but can't access premium buyers.

New mechanics: certification tiers, audit events, premium price multipliers, compliance score
